- **Source**: Janssens GE, Trętowicz MM, Grevendonk L, et al. "Delayed molecular aging, preservation of energy metabolism and enhanced exercise response in exercise-trained human muscle." *Nature Aging* 6, 1482–1500 (2026). DOI: [10.1038/s43587-026-01150-x](https://doi.org/10.1038/s43587-026-01150-x)
- **One-liner**: Regular daily movement is valuable, but sustained, structured exercise training may preserve the energy-producing machinery of aging muscle better than movement alone.

- ## Findings
	- Older adults who took as many daily steps as young adults still showed age-related declines in mitochondrial energy-production genes, along with lower NAD+ and more stored fat in muscle.
	- In exercise-trained older adults, roughly half of the age-associated gene-expression differences were absent. The strongest preserved pathways were mitochondrial respiration and energy metabolism.
	- Training also preserved a stronger, more youthful stress response to an exercise session. This short-lived inflammatory response may be part of adaptation rather than harmful chronic inflammation.
	- Some nerve-signaling and tissue-maintenance changes remained despite training, so exercise does not prevent every aspect of muscle aging.

- ## Actions for Muscle Health
	- **Do structured training, not only daily steps.**
		- In this study, “trained” meant at least **three planned, one-hour exercise sessions per week for more than one year**.
		- Structured training means deliberate workouts that repeatedly challenge the body enough to create adaptation—for example resistance training, brisk aerobic sessions, swimming, cycling, exercise classes, or sports.
		- This does not have to happen in a gym. A scheduled brisk walk can count when it is performed as a purposeful workout rather than accumulated incidentally.
	- **Keep moving outside workouts.**
		- Walking, stairs, chores, and other daily movement remain beneficial; the study suggests they complement rather than replace training.
	- **Train consistently for the long term.**
		- The relevant difference was sustained training over years, not a short burst of exercise. Choose activities that are challenging but realistic enough to maintain.
	- **Include a progressive challenge and recovery.**
		- Gradually increase resistance, pace, duration, or difficulty as fitness improves, then allow recovery. Step counts alone do not capture this training stimulus.
	- **Do not try to eliminate every post-exercise inflammatory response.**
		- A temporary stress response after exercise is normal and may help drive adaptation. This finding is not a reason to stop medically prescribed anti-inflammatory treatment.

- ## Important Caveat
	- The study was small and cross-sectional: it compared people who already trained with people who did not, rather than randomly assigning a training program. It therefore shows a strong association, not definitive causation.
	- “Half” refers to measured gene-expression differences—not half of muscle loss, disability, or overall biological aging.
	- Three one-hour sessions per week was the study’s group definition, not a proven minimum or optimal prescription.
